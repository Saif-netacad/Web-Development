Creating List Of My favourite Book


  1. To Kill a Mockingbird

    Author:Herper Lee

    Description: it tells the story of Scout Finch, a young girl whose father, lawyer Atticus Finch, defends a black man falsely accused of raping a white woman. The novel explores themes of racial injustice and moral growth.

  2. 1984

    Author:George Orwell

    Description:A dystopian novel set in a totalitarian future where government surveillance, propaganda, and thought control are pervasive. It follows Winston Smith, who rebels against the oppressive regime led by Big Brother.

  3. The Great Gatsby

    Author:F. Scott Fitzgerald

    Descriptin:Set in the Jazz Age of the 1920s, the novel portrays the glamorous and decadent lifestyle of Jay Gatsby, a wealthy man who tries to win back his lost love, Daisy Buchanan. It explores themes of wealth, love, and the American Dream.

  4. Pride and Prejudice

    Author:Jane Austen

    Descrption:A classic romance novel set in early 19th-century England, it follows Elizabeth Bennet, one of five sisters, as she navigates the social norms of her time and her complicated relationship with the wealthy and aloof Mr. Darcy.

  5. One Hundred Years of Solitude

    Author:Gabriel García Márquez

    Description:A landmark of magic realism, the novel chronicles the Buendía family over several generations in the fictional town of Macondo. It blends magical elements with historical events, exploring themes of love, power, and the cyclical nature of history.
